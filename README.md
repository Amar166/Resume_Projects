# Resume_Projects
A portfolio of my VLSI projects completed through coursework and independent learning. It includes LDO regulators, charge pumps, oscillators, analog and digital circuit designs, and Verilog implementations, with design files, simulation results, and technical documentation.
The Negative - Voltage LDO Project is the current project that I am persuing from 14th of June till the present date and I have attached the slides that indicate progress on the project from Day - 1.
Designed Low Dropout (LDO) regulator is intended to provide a stable 1.6 V regulated output from a 1.8 V input supply while supporting load currents ranging from 20 mA to 100 mA (50 mA nominal). The design emphasizes high power efficiency (≥88.45%), good power supply rejection (PSRR of approx −40 dB at 100 kHz), and accurate voltage regulation with a DC output error of less than ±0.8 mV. The design uses a maximum 500 pF output capacitor and a 8 pF compensation capacitor. It also achieves tight line and load regulation, maintaining output stability under variations in supply voltage and load current. Furthermore, the LDO is designed to deliver a fast transient response, limiting output voltage overshoot and undershoot to 150 mV with a settling time below 155 ns, while maintaining reliable performance at 27 deg.
